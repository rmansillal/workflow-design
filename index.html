BUSI4652 - Generating Customer Insights and Analytics

Digital Learning Experience Design for Customer Segmentation with Clustering Analysis

This document outlines a 3.5-hour digital learning experience for the module BUSI4652, 'Generating Customer Insights and Analytics'. The experience is designed to develop students' conceptual understanding and practical skills in clustering analysis for marketing applications.

Foundational Pedagogical Principles:
Constructive Alignment (Biggs, 1996): As established in my previous coursework submitted for the module 'Curriculum Design and Assessment in Higher Education', the principle of constructive alignment is the core foundation for this learning design. The Learning Outcomes (LOs) for this session are explicitly aligned with the Teaching and Learning Activities (TLAs) and the formative assessment tasks embedded within the session. Every activity is purposefully designed to help students achieve the stated outcomes.
Conversational Framework (Laurillard, 2012): To structure the learning process, I have used Laurillard's Conversational Framework. My design ensures students cycle through all six of Laurillard's learning types: Acquisition, Enquiry, Practice, Discussion, Collaboration, and Production, creating a multi-faceted learning journey.

Learning Outcomes:

  • LO1: Explain the conceptual basis of clustering and differentiate between major clustering techniques.
  • LO2: Apply K-Means clustering analysis to datasets using statistical software.
  • LO3: Interpret analysis outputs to create data-driven customer personas and inform decision-making.
